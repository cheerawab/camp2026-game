# Pebble Open Power: Points / Achievement System Context

## Project Goal

This is a gamified system for an information-education camp such as SITCON Camp.

Although it may look like a “points system,” the intended direction is closer to:

- an achievement system
- a social lubricant
- a team contribution system
- a feedback layer for course and activity participation
- a lightweight record of each learner’s camp journey

The system should not be designed as a pure numerical score, leaderboard race, or idle game.

The main purpose is not to let participants farm points.  
The purpose is to gently push participants toward behaviors we want to encourage.

---

## Behaviors We Want to Encourage

The system should reward behaviors such as:

1. Community booth interaction
   - Visiting booths
   - Asking questions
   - Talking with community members

2. Course focus and reflection
   - Paying attention in class
   - Asking questions
   - Answering questions
   - Taking notes
   - Sharing one thing they learned after a SIG or course session

3. Curiosity and exploration
   - Finding unexpected solutions
   - Discovering shortcuts for assignments
   - Sharing interesting discoveries with others

4. Social interaction and cross-team collaboration
   - Helping teammates
   - Collaborating across teams
   - Talking to people they are not familiar with

5. Team contribution
   - Helping the team progress toward shared goals
   - Allowing lower-achievement, shy, or slower-to-warm-up members to contribute meaningfully

6. Feedback and camp participation
   - Filling out feedback forms
   - Joining recreational activities
   - Completing tasks that the camp wants participants to experience

---

## Behaviors We Should Avoid Over-Rewarding

Avoid designing the system to reward:

- Pure answer speed
- Raw number of solved questions
- Tasks that can be farmed alone in a corner
- Tasks that are only easy for high-achievement participants
- Excessive competition or comparison
- Any mechanism that interrupts courses or activities
- Any design that makes participants feel they can no longer catch up
- Any design that creates a “point billionaire vs. socially isolated participant” gap
